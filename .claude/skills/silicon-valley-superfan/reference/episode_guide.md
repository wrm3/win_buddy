# Episode Guide - Silicon Valley (All 53 Episodes)

## Season 1 (2014) - "The Compression"

### S1E1 - "Minimum Viable Product" (April 6, 2014)
- **Director**: Mike Judge | **Writer**: Mike Judge, John Altschuler, Dave Krinsky
- **Plot**: Richard works at Hooli, creates compression algorithm as side project. Peter Gregory and Gavin Belson both want it. Richard chooses Peter's $200K over Gavin's $10M.
- **Key Moments**: First appearance of all main characters. Erlich's "Aviato" introduction. The incubator pitch. Gavin's "making the world a better place" speech at Hooli.
- **Notable Quote**: "I don't want to live in a world where someone else makes the world a better place better than we do." -- Gavin Belson
- **Tech Detail**: Introduces middle-out compression concept. Weissman score first mentioned.
- **Guest Stars**: Series premiere, full cast introduction

### S1E2 - "The Cap Table" (April 13, 2014)
- **Director**: Mike Judge | **Writer**: Carson Mell
- **Plot**: Erlich demands equity restructuring. Peter Gregory's bizarre investment style revealed (sesame seeds → Burger King → Indonesian agriculture). Big Head gets promoted at Hooli for being friends with Richard.
- **Key Moments**: Peter Gregory's sesame seed investment insight. First "Big Head fails upward" moment. Dinesh's chain introduced.
- **Notable Quote**: "Let me ask you something. How fast do you think you could jerk off every guy in this room?" -- Erlich (foreshadowing the S1 finale)

### S1E3 - "Articles of Incorporation" (April 20, 2014)
- **Director**: Tricia Brock | **Writer**: Matteo Borghese, Rob Turbovsky
- **Plot**: Richard needs a company name. "Pied Piper" is taken by a sprinkler company. Jared gets trapped in a self-driving car. Gilfoyle and Dinesh rivalry established.
- **Key Moments**: The name Pied Piper chosen. Jared's self-driving car nightmare (ends up on an island). First major Gilfoyle-Dinesh exchange.
- **Notable Quote**: "RIGBY! RIGBY!" -- Jared (the safe word for the self-driving car that doesn't work)

### S1E4 - "Fiduciary Duties" (April 27, 2014)
- **Director**: Maggie Carey | **Writer**: Matteo Borghese, Rob Turbovsky
- **Plot**: Peter Gregory tasks Monica with due diligence on Pied Piper. Richard struggles with being a leader. Erlich's past business failures revealed.
- **Key Moments**: Peter Gregory's eccentricities deepen. Monica begins to believe in Pied Piper. First hints of Jared's dark backstory.

### S1E5 - "Signaling Risk" (May 4, 2014)
- **Director**: Mike Judge | **Writer**: Dan O'Keefe
- **Plot**: Pied Piper needs to present at TechCrunch Disrupt but Richard can't public speak. Erlich steps in as the face. Gavin launches "Nucleus" to compete.
- **Key Moments**: Richard's stage fright is debilitating. Erlich proves his value as a frontman. Nucleus project launched at Hooli.
- **Notable Quote**: "You know what has 'also' in it? Also-ran." -- Erlich

### S1E6 - "Third Party Insourcing" (May 11, 2014)
- **Director**: Maggie Carey | **Writer**: Jessica Gao
- **Plot**: Pied Piper hires contractors. One of them introduces a virus/security issue. Gilfoyle's security paranoia justified.
- **Key Moments**: Gilfoyle's server security expertise showcased. The contractor subplot mirrors real startup growing pains.

### S1E7 - "Proof of Concept" (May 18, 2014)
- **Director**: Mike Judge | **Writer**: Dan O'Keefe
- **Plot**: TechCrunch Disrupt preparations. Pied Piper needs to prove the algorithm works at scale. Richard realizes their demo will fail against EndFrame's presentation.
- **Key Moments**: EndFrame revealed as major competitor. Stakes for TechCrunch Disrupt established. Peter Gregory's last major appearance.
- **Dedication**: To Christopher Evan Welch (Peter Gregory) who passed away during filming

### S1E8 - "Optimal Tip-to-Tip Efficiency" (June 1, 2014) ★ ICONIC EPISODE
- **Director**: Mike Judge | **Writer**: Alec Berg
- **Plot**: During Erlich's party, the guys calculate the optimal method for mass-stimulation (the "dick joke"). Richard has his eureka moment -- the math maps to middle-out compression. Pied Piper wins TechCrunch Disrupt.
- **Key Moments**: THE whiteboard scene. The mathematical proof is real and was validated by Stanford researchers. Pied Piper achieves a Weissman score of 5.2, beating EndFrame. 
- **Notable Quote**: "We can get to 800... in ten minutes." -- Richard (realizing the compression breakthrough)
- **Tech Detail**: The "Mean Jerk Time" optimization is a legitimate operations research problem. The show hired real mathematicians to ensure the whiteboard math was correct.
- **Cultural Impact**: This episode's whiteboard scene became one of the most famous moments in TV comedy. The actual paper was published.

## Season 2 (2015) - "The Pivot"

### S2E1 - "Sand Hill Shuffle" (April 12, 2015)
- **Plot**: Post-TechCrunch victory. Pied Piper needs more funding. Russ Hanneman introduced ("I put radio on the internet"). Peter Gregory's death handled off-screen. Laurie Bream introduced.
- **Key Moments**: Russ Hanneman's iconic entrance. "Three comma club" established. Laurie Bream's emotionless management style.
- **Notable Quote**: "This guy fucks!" -- Russ Hanneman (about Jared, becomes his catchphrase)

### S2E2 - "Runaway Devaluation" (April 19, 2015)
- **Plot**: Russ Hanneman's investment causes problems. Valuation debates. Dinesh gets a gold chain.
- **Key Moments**: The chain debuts. "Does it make me look like a Pakistani Mr. T?"

### S2E3 - "Bad Money" (April 26, 2015)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper tries to pivot. Russ Hanneman keeps interfering. Gavin's Nucleus continues to fail.
- **Notable Quote**: "Do you know what 'RIGBY' stands for? Richard Is Great, Bye." -- No one says this, but Jared would

### S2E4 - "The Lady" (May 3, 2015)
- **Plot**: Carla Walton joins as the first female programmer. The team's brogrammer culture is exposed. Dinesh is smitten.
- **Key Moments**: Gender dynamics in tech satirized. Carla immediately outperforms Dinesh.

### S2E5 - "Server Space" (May 10, 2015)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper needs server infrastructure. Gilfoyle builds custom servers. Data center costs are astronomical.
- **Key Moments**: Gilfoyle's server expertise becomes plot-critical. Real-world infrastructure costs depicted accurately.

### S2E6 - "Homicide" (May 17, 2015)
- **Plot**: Richard gets fired as CEO by the board. Devastating for him but the board cites his leadership failures. Becomes CTO instead.
- **Key Moments**: Richard losing CEO position. Power dynamics shift. First major "business beats genius" lesson.

### S2E7 - "Adult Content" (May 24, 2015)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper's platform accidentally becomes popular for adult content. They debate whether to keep it.
- **Key Moments**: Classic tech industry dilemma -- your platform is being used for something you didn't intend. Tumblr/OnlyFans parallels.

### S2E8 - "White Hat/Black Hat" (May 31, 2015)
- **Plot**: Security breach at Pied Piper. Gilfoyle investigates. Seth (hacker) introduced.
- **Key Moments**: Cybersecurity depicted relatively accurately. Gilfoyle in his element.

### S2E9 - "Binding Arbitration" (June 7, 2015)
- **Plot**: Hooli sues Pied Piper, claiming Richard developed the algorithm on Hooli time. Arbitration proceedings.
- **Key Moments**: Legal drama satirized. The "on company time" IP ownership question is a real Silicon Valley concern.

### S2E10 - "Two Days of the Condor" (June 14, 2015)
- **Plot**: Season finale. Pied Piper wins the lawsuit. But Russ Hanneman's interference causes a data loss crisis. Everything crashes. Cliffhanger.
- **Key Moments**: Victory snatched away at the last second (recurring theme). Russ Hanneman's "three commas" net worth tracker shows he drops below $1B.

## Season 3 (2016) - "The Platform"

### S3E1 - "Founder Friendly" (April 24, 2016)
- **Plot**: Aftermath of S2 finale. Action Jack Barker brought in as CEO. Richard is sidelined.
- **Key Moments**: Jack Barker's "Conjoined Triangles of Success" introduced. Corporate culture clash begins.

### S3E2 - "Two in the Box" (May 1, 2016)
- **Plot**: Jack Barker pivots Pied Piper to building a hardware "box." Richard rebels internally.

### S3E3 - "Meinertzhagen's Haversack" (May 8, 2016)
- **Plot**: Richard plots to undermine Jack Barker. Gilfoyle and Dinesh develop Skunkworks project.
- **Named After**: A real WWII deception tactic. The show loves obscure references.

### S3E4 - "Maleant Data Systems Solutions" (May 15, 2016)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper partners with a data solutions company. Corporate meetings are soul-crushing.

### S3E5 - "The Empty Chair" (May 22, 2016)
- **Plot**: Peter Gregory's legacy debated. His empty chair at Raviga represents his absence. Laurie solidifies power.

### S3E6 - "Bachmanity Insanity" (May 29, 2016)
- **Plot**: Erlich and Big Head's "Bachmanity" fund hosts a launch party on Alcatraz. It fails spectacularly. The party is a disaster but somehow helps Pied Piper.
- **Key Moments**: Bachmanity's Alcatraz party is peak Erlich content. Kid Rock performs.

### S3E7 - "To Build a Better Beta" (June 5, 2016)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper beta launches. User testing reveals problems. Richard's perfectionism vs. shipping.

### S3E8 - "Bachman's Earning's Over-Ride" (June 12, 2016)
- **Plot**: Erlich tries to leverage his remaining equity. Board politics intensify.

### S3E9 - "Daily Active Users" (June 19, 2016)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper struggles to get daily active users. Growth hacking attempts fail. Richard considers fake users.
- **Key Moments**: The DAU (Daily Active Users) metric obsession satirizes real startup culture.

### S3E10 - "The Uptick" (June 26, 2016)
- **Plot**: Season finale. Jack Barker ousted. Richard retakes control. Pivots to "new internet" concept. Sets up Season 4's decentralized internet arc.
- **Key Moments**: Richard's vision for a decentralized internet born. The show presciently predicted the blockchain/Web3 movement.

## Season 4 (2017) - "The New Internet"

### S4E1 - "Success Failure" (April 23, 2017)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper builds PiperNet. Decentralized internet concept introduced. Jian-Yang's "Not Hotdog" app development begins.

### S4E2 - "Terms of Service" (April 30, 2017)
- **Plot**: Legal complications with the new internet. Terms of service satire. Jian-Yang's app progresses.

### S4E3 - "Intellectual Property" (May 7, 2017)
- **Plot**: IP concerns with PiperNet. Who owns the decentralized internet?

### S4E4 - "Teambuilding Exercise" (May 14, 2017)
- **Plot**: The team does a forced teambuilding exercise. It goes poorly. Gilfoyle and Dinesh's dynamic explored.

### S4E5 - "The Blood Boy" (May 21, 2017) ★ FAN FAVORITE
- **Plot**: Gavin Belson gets a "blood boy" -- a young person whose blood is transfused to him for vitality. Real practice satirized (Peter Thiel reportedly explored parabiosis).
- **Key Moments**: Blood boy concept becomes iconic. Gavin's desperation for youth. This satirized real Silicon Valley anti-aging obsession.
- **Notable Quote**: "I am the blood boy."

### S4E6 - "Customer Service" (May 28, 2017)
- **Plot**: PiperNet gets its first users but customer service nightmares begin.

### S4E7 - "The Patent Troll" (June 4, 2017)
- **Plot**: A patent troll targets Pied Piper. Satire of the patent troll industry.

### S4E8 - "The Keenan Vortex" (June 11, 2017)
- **Plot**: Keenan Feldspar (a VR startup founder, satirizing Palmer Luckey/Oculus) becomes involved. VR hype satirized.
- **Key Moments**: VR bubble satire years before the metaverse crash

### S4E9 - "Hooli-Con" (June 18, 2017)
- **Plot**: Hooli's annual conference. Gavin tries to launch his internet. Pied Piper plots to undermine him.
- **Key Moments**: Tech conference culture satirized perfectly.

### S4E10 - "Server Error" (June 25, 2017)
- **Plot**: Season finale. Erlich's departure (goes to Tibet). Jian-Yang declares Erlich dead. PiperNet launch complications. ICO (Initial Coin Offering) storyline begins.
- **Key Moments**: T.J. Miller's final episode. Erlich left at an opium den in Tibet. Gavin pays for one year. The ICO parallels the real crypto boom of 2017.

## Season 5 (2018) - "The Growth"

### S5E1 - "Grow Fast or Die Slow" (March 25, 2018)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper has 500K daily users but needs to scale. Post-Erlich era. Richard becomes more ruthless.

### S5E2 - "Reorientation" (April 1, 2018)
- **Plot**: Team reorientation without Erlich. Jared takes on more responsibility. Dinesh's chain evolution continues.

### S5E3 - "Chief Operating Officer" (April 8, 2018)
- **Plot**: Jared's COO role formalized. His dark past hinted at more. "When I was in my fourth foster home..."

### S5E4 - "Tech Evangelist" (April 15, 2018)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper needs a tech evangelist. Hiring process satirized.

### S5E5 - "Facial Recognition" (April 22, 2018)
- **Plot**: Facial recognition technology ethics debated. The algorithm can't recognize some faces. Bias in AI satirized.
- **Key Moments**: Prescient commentary on AI bias that became a major real-world issue in following years.

### S5E6 - "Artificial Emotional Intelligence" (April 29, 2018)
- **Plot**: AI ethics continue. Gilfoyle's Anton becomes more sophisticated.

### S5E7 - "Initial Coin Offering" (May 6, 2018)
- **Plot**: Pied Piper considers an ICO. Crypto satire. Real-world parallels to the 2017-2018 ICO boom/bust.

### S5E8 - "Fifty-One Percent" (May 13, 2018)
- **Plot**: Season finale. 51% attack on PiperNet. Laurie Bream's true nature revealed. Gilfoyle saves the day. Setup for final season.
- **Key Moments**: 51% attack is a real blockchain vulnerability. Gilfoyle's technical save is one of his finest moments.

## Season 6 (2019) - "The End"

### S6E1 - "Artificial Lack of Intelligence" (October 27, 2019)
- **Plot**: Time skip. Pied Piper is growing. Richard discovers a flaw in his algorithm that could break all encryption.
- **Key Moments**: The central moral dilemma of the final season introduced. AI becoming dangerous.

### S6E2 - "Blood Money" (November 3, 2019)
- **Plot**: Richard tries to fix the encryption flaw secretly. The team doesn't know. Gavin Belson fired from Hooli.

### S6E3 - "Hooli Smokes!" (November 10, 2019)
- **Plot**: Hooli in crisis post-Gavin. Amazon acquisition rumors. Gavin becomes a professor at Tethics.
- **Key Moments**: "Tethics" (tech ethics) coined. Gavin teaching ethics is the height of irony.

### S6E4 - "Maximizing Alphaness" (November 17, 2019)
- **Plot**: Richard's secret eats at him. The team begins to suspect something. Internal conflict rises.

### S6E5 - "Tethics" (November 24, 2019)
- **Plot**: Tech ethics conference. Gavin's "Tethics" becomes a movement. Richard confronts the moral implications.

### S6E6 - "RussFest" (December 1, 2019)
- **Plot**: Russ Hanneman throws a massive festival. It's Fyre Festival meets Burning Man. Chaos ensues.
- **Key Moments**: Fyre Festival satire (came out right after the documentaries). Russ at his most Russ.

### S6E7 - "Exit Event" (December 8, 2019) ★ SERIES FINALE
- **Plot**: Richard deliberately introduces a bug that causes Pied Piper to fail at its biggest demo. He sacrifices the company to prevent his algorithm from breaking all internet encryption. Flash-forward shows: Richard is a CS professor at Stanford, Gilfoyle works security, Dinesh works at a smaller company (still wearing the chain), Jared is a successful CEO, Monica runs her own fund, Big Head is still inexplicably successful, and Gavin drives a Lyft.
- **Key Moments**: 
  - Richard's sacrifice echoes the pilot -- he chooses principle over money again
  - The flash-forward montage is both satisfying and bittersweet
  - Gilfoyle's silent nod to Richard suggests he knew and approved
  - Jian-Yang is still thriving in China with YaoNet
  - The fake-out: everyone thinks Pied Piper will finally succeed, then Richard tanks it
- **Notable Quote**: "I'm not going to be the person that destroys the internet." -- Richard
- **Series Final Shot**: Richard teaching a Stanford class, drawing the middle-out compression diagram on a whiteboard, full circle from the pilot
- **Cultural Impact**: Praised as one of the best series finales in comedy history. Avoided the Game of Thrones problem (which aired the same year on HBO).
