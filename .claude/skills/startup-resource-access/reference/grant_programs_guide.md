# Grant Programs Guide

## SBIR/STTR Overview

**SBIR (Small Business Innovation Research)**:
- $4B+ awarded annually across 11 agencies
- Non-dilutive funding
- Three phases

**Phase I**: $50k-$250k (6-12 months) - Feasibility
**Phase II**: $750k-$1.5M (24 months) - Development  
**Phase III**: Commercialization (no SBIR funds)

**Agencies**: NIH, DOD, DOE, NSF, NASA, USDA, DOT, DHS, EPA, NOAA, Dept of Education

**Eligibility**:
- US-owned and operated
- <500 employees
- For-profit small business
- 51%+ owned by US citizens

**Success Rates**: 15-25% (Phase I), 40-50% (Phase II)

## Application Process

**Timeline**: 6-12 months from application to funding

**Steps**:
1. Find relevant solicitation
2. Read requirements carefully
3. Prepare proposal (100+ hours)
4. Submit through agency portal
5. Wait for reviews (3-6 months)
6. Award notification

**Proposal Components**:
- Technical approach (10-15 pages)
- Commercialization plan (5-10 pages)
- Budget and justification
- Team biosketches

**Best Practices**:
- Align with agency mission
- Strong commercialization plan
- Show technical innovation
- Demonstrate team capability
- Use consultants for first application

## Major Grant Programs

**NIH SBIR** (Health/Biomedical):
- Phase I: $300k
- Phase II: $1.8M
- Focus areas: NCI, NHLBI, NIAID

**NSF SBIR** (Deep Tech):
- Phase I: $256k
- Phase II: $1M
- Focus: AI/ML, biotech, quantum, climate

**NSF I-Corps**:
- $50k for customer discovery
- 6-8 week program
- Perfect for early-stage

**DOE SBIR** (Energy):
- Phase I: $200k
- Phase II: $1.15M
- Focus: Energy, grid, renewables

**State Grants**: $10k-$500k (varies by state)

## Grant Application Strategy

**Before Applying**:
- Assess fit with agency mission
- Read past successful awards
- Talk to program officers
- Budget 100+ hours

**Common Mistakes**:
- Wrong agency/topic
- Weak commercialization plan
- Overly ambitious timeline
- Poor writing
- No preliminary data
