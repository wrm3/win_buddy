# Production Details - Silicon Valley

## Creators and Showrunners

### Mike Judge (Co-Creator, Director, Writer, EP)
- Created Beavis and Butt-Head, King of the Hill, Office Space, Idiocracy
- Worked as an engineer at a Silicon Valley startup before Hollywood
- His real-world tech experience informed the show's authenticity
- Directed the pilot and multiple key episodes
- Provided the voice for some background characters
- Left day-to-day showrunning after Season 1 but remained involved throughout

### John Altschuler (Co-Creator, Writer, EP)
- Long-time collaborator with Mike Judge
- Co-wrote King of the Hill
- Departed after Season 2 to pursue other projects

### Dave Krinsky (Co-Creator, Writer, EP)
- Other half of the Altschuler/Krinsky writing team
- Also departed after Season 2

### Alec Berg (Showrunner S2-6, Writer, EP)
- Took over as showrunner from Season 2 onward
- Previously wrote for Seinfeld and Curb Your Enthusiasm
- His comedy sensibility shaped the show's later seasons
- Also created Barry (with Bill Hader) while running Silicon Valley simultaneously
- Considered the unsung hero of the show's consistency

### Christopher Evan Welch's Passing
- Welch was diagnosed with lung cancer during Season 1 filming
- He passed away on December 2, 2013, before the show aired
- The show's creators called him "the best actor no one had heard of"
- His performance as Peter Gregory in just 7 episodes remains one of the show's most beloved elements
- S1E7 was dedicated to his memory
- The character's death was handled off-screen with dignity
- Laurie Bream was written as his successor, deliberately different in every way to honor the character

### T.J. Miller's Departure
- Left after Season 4 (2017)
- Mix of creative differences and personal controversies
- The show handled his exit by sending Erlich to Tibet
- Miller later faced allegations of bullying and assault
- The show continued successfully without him, though his energy was missed
- The writers never brought the character back, even for the finale

### The Hacker Hostel Set
- Built on a soundstage at Sony Pictures Studios in Culver City
- The living room/workspace was designed by production designer Richard Toyon
- Erlich's bong collection was created by a custom glass artist
- The whiteboard in the living room contained real (but wrong) equations as jokes
- Gilfoyle's server rack was built with actual server components
- The set was deliberately cluttered to feel "lived in"
- The garage was a separate set piece that could be rearranged

### Hooli Campus
- Exteriors shot at various corporate campuses in the LA area
- Interiors were built on soundstages
- The "Gavin Belson Signature Edition" products were real props
- The Consideration Garden was inspired by real tech CEO meditation spaces
- Hooli's lobby changed decor each season to reflect corporate rebranding

### TechCrunch Disrupt
- The Season 1 finale's TechCrunch Disrupt scenes were filmed at a convention center
- TechCrunch gave permission to use their actual brand name
- The competition format was based on real Disrupt events
- Some extras were actual tech conference attendees

## Music and Soundtrack

### Theme Song
- Composed by **Tobacco** (Thomas Fec of Black Moth Super Rainbow)
- The electronic/chiptune theme became instantly recognizable
- Didn't change across all 6 seasons
- The accompanying title sequence showed Silicon Valley landmarks with a tech overlay

### Soundtrack Highlights
- The show used a mix of electronic, hip-hop, and indie music
- Russ Hanneman scenes often featured loud, brash music reflecting his personality
- The "Tres Commas" tequila brand got its own jingle
- Season 1 finale used "Stretch Your Face" by Tobacco
- The series finale used a carefully curated playlist for emotional impact

### Notable Music Moments
- Erlich's TechCrunch pitch is accompanied by triumphant music
- Gavin Belson's entrance music at Hooli events parodies Apple keynote scoring
- Ron LaFlamme constantly references real bands (Ratt, etc.)

## Production Design

### Richard Toyon (Production Designer)
- Created the Hacker Hostel as the show's most iconic space
- Designed Hooli's campus to parody real tech campuses
- Each character's workspace reflected their personality:
  - Richard: Messy, scattered papers, laptop dominant
  - Gilfoyle: Dark, organized, server equipment prominent
  - Dinesh: Neat but cluttered with personal items and the chain
  - Jared: Color-coded folders, organized post-its, Kanban board
  - Erlich: Bongs, Aviato memorabilia, grandiose but tacky

### Prop Details
- Erlich's bong collection contained 12+ custom pieces
- The Pied Piper logo went through several redesigns (matching the in-show rebrandings)
- Gavin's office contained a real commissioned portrait of himself
- The "blood boy" equipment was medically accurate
- Russ Hanneman's car collection used real exotic vehicles
- The Aviato billboard was a physical prop installed in the LA area

### Costume Design
- Each character had a distinct "uniform":
  - Richard: Red/blue flannel, hoodies (getting nicer as he gains power)
  - Gilfoyle: All black, heavy metal band shirts
  - Dinesh: Collared shirts, the gold chain, attempt at fashionable
  - Jared: Button-down shirts, khakis (office appropriate even at a startup)
  - Erlich: Aviato shirts, Hawaiian shirts, "startup bro" aesthetic
  - Gavin: Expensive but understated (Steve Jobs turtleneck energy)
  - Russ Hanneman: Flashy, designer, logos visible, "new money"
