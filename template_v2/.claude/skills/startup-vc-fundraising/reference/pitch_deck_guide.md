# Pitch Deck Creation Guide

Comprehensive slide-by-slide guide to creating a compelling investor pitch deck.

## Executive Summary

**Purpose**: Communicate your business vision and opportunity clearly and compellingly to investors

**Format**: 15-slide presentation (PDF for sharing, PPT for presenting)

**Duration**: 15-20 minute presentation, 10-15 minutes for Q&A

**Distribution**: Email as PDF with password protection, upload to DocSend for tracking

## The 15-Slide Framework

### Slide 1: Cover Slide

**Purpose**: First impression, professional presentation, clear identity

**Elements to Include**:
- Company logo (prominent, top center)
- Company name
- Tagline or one-line value proposition
- "Confidential - For Discussion Purposes Only"
- Date (month/year)
- Founder name(s) and title
- Contact information (email, phone)
- Website URL

**Design Tips**:
- Clean, professional design
- High-quality imagery or brand visual
- Consistent with brand identity
- Not too cluttered

**Example**:
```
[LOGO]

Acme Analytics
AI-Powered Revenue Intelligence for B2B Sales Teams

Seed Round - October 2025
Jane Doe, CEO | jane@acmeanalytics.com
www.acmeanalytics.com

Confidential - For Discussion Purposes Only
```

**Common Mistakes**:
- Missing contact information
- No confidentiality notice
- Cluttered or unprofessional design
- Forgetting to date the deck

### Slide 2: The Problem

**Purpose**: Hook investors with a clear, painful problem that needs solving

**Structure**:
1. One-sentence problem statement (bold, large font)
2. Who experiences this problem?
3. How painful/expensive is it? (quantify)
4. Current solutions and their shortcomings
5. Why existing solutions fail

**Storytelling Approach**:
- Start with customer pain point
- Use real-world example or anecdote
- Quantify the impact (time, money, frustration)
- Make it relatable and visceral

**Example (SaaS)**:
```
PROBLEM: B2B sales teams waste 40% of their time on non-selling activities

Who's affected?
‚Ä¢ 6M+ B2B sales reps globally
‚Ä¢ Sales leaders at growth companies ($10M-$100M ARR)

Pain points:
‚Ä¢ Sales reps spend only 3 hours/day actually selling
‚Ä¢ CRM data entry takes 2+ hours daily
‚Ä¢ Manual forecasting is inaccurate (¬±30%)
‚Ä¢ Deals slip due to lack of buyer insights

Current solutions fail:
‚Ä¢ Generic CRM tools lack sales-specific intelligence
‚Ä¢ Business intelligence tools require data science expertise
‚Ä¢ Manual processes don't scale
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Pain point icons or images
- Customer quote or testimonial
- Data visualization (chart/graph)
- Before/after comparison

**Common Mistakes**:
- Problem too vague or generic
- No quantification of pain
- Solution-first instead of problem-first
- Ignoring existing alternatives

### Slide 3: The Solution

**Purpose**: Present your product as the obvious, elegant solution to the problem

**Structure**:
1. One-sentence solution statement
2. How it works (simple explanation)
3. Key benefits (3-5 bullets)
4. Why now? (market timing, technology enablers)
5. Visual (screenshot, diagram, or workflow)

**Positioning**:
- Position as 10x better, not 10% better
- Focus on outcomes, not features
- Explain "secret sauce" or unique approach
- Connect directly to problem on previous slide

**Example**:
```
SOLUTION: AI-powered revenue intelligence that automatically forecasts deals and coaches sellers

How it works:
‚Ä¢ Integrates with email, calendar, CRM, and calls
‚Ä¢ AI analyzes every customer interaction
‚Ä¢ Provides real-time deal insights and coaching
‚Ä¢ Automates CRM updates and forecasting

Key benefits:
‚Ä¢ 40% more time selling (automated admin work)
‚Ä¢ 95% forecast accuracy (vs. 70% manual)
‚Ä¢ 25% higher win rates (AI-powered coaching)
‚Ä¢ Zero data entry required

Why now?
‚Ä¢ AI/LLM breakthroughs enable natural language understanding
‚Ä¢ Sales teams now fully remote, need digital insights
‚Ä¢ Buying committees larger (6+ people), harder to track
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Product screenshot (clean, annotated)
- Before/after comparison
- Simple workflow diagram
- Customer success metric

**Common Mistakes**:
- Too technical or feature-focused
- No clear differentiation
- Forgetting "why now"
- Solution doesn't match problem

### Slide 4: Market Size

**Purpose**: Prove there's a massive, growing opportunity worth investing in

**Structure**:
1. TAM (Total Addressable Market): Global opportunity
2. SAM (Serviceable Addressable Market): Realistically reachable
3. SOM (Serviceable Obtainable Market): Your realistic target
4. Market growth rate and trends
5. Bottom-up calculation methodology

**TAM/SAM/SOM Framework**:

**TAM**: Total global market size
- Example: "6M B2B sales reps √ó $10k/rep/year = $60B TAM"

**SAM**: Subset you can realistically serve
- Example: "Growth companies (500-5k employees) = 2M reps = $20B SAM"

**SOM**: What you can capture in 3-5 years
- Example: "5% market share = $1B revenue opportunity"

**Calculation Methods**:
1. **Top-down**: Industry reports, analyst firms
2. **Bottom-up**: Target customers √ó price point
3. **Value theory**: Willingness to pay √ó number of users

**Example**:
```
MARKET OPPORTUNITY: $60B+ and growing 20% annually

TAM: $60B
‚Ä¢ 6M B2B sales reps globally
‚Ä¢ $10k average spend per rep annually
‚Ä¢ Sales technology spending growing 20% YoY

SAM: $20B
‚Ä¢ Focus on growth companies (500-5k employees)
‚Ä¢ 2M sales reps in target segment
‚Ä¢ Higher willingness to pay ($12k/rep)

SOM: $1B+ in 5 years
‚Ä¢ Target 5% market share by year 5
‚Ä¢ 80k customers √ó $12k = $960M ARR potential

Bottom-up validation:
‚Ä¢ 500k target companies globally
‚Ä¢ 15% sales technology adoption = 75k potential customers
‚Ä¢ $15k ACV = $1.1B opportunity
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Concentric circles (TAM/SAM/SOM)
- Market growth chart
- Geographic or segment breakdown
- Analyst firm logos/quotes

**Common Mistakes**:
- TAM too small (< $1B red flag)
- Only top-down (no bottom-up validation)
- Unrealistic SOM (> 30% of SAM)
- No growth rate or trends
- Forgetting to show math

### Slide 5: Product Demo

**Purpose**: Show, don't just tell - visualize your product and its value

**Structure**:
1. 3-4 key screenshots or product images
2. Annotate key features and benefits
3. Show user workflow or customer journey
4. Highlight differentiation
5. Link to video demo (optional)

**What to Show**:
- Core value proposition in action
- User interface and experience
- Key features that differentiate
- Real customer data (anonymized)
- Before/after comparison

**Screenshot Selection**:
1. **Dashboard/Overview**: What users see first
2. **Key Feature**: Your differentiator in action
3. **Insights/Output**: Value delivered
4. **Workflow**: How it fits into user's day

**Example (Sales Intelligence Tool)**:
```
[Screenshot 1: Dashboard]
AI Deal Dashboard - Real-time health scores for all opportunities

[Screenshot 2: Deal Insights]
Conversation Intelligence - AI analyzes calls and emails for risks

[Screenshot 3: Forecasting]
Automated Forecast - 95% accurate, updated in real-time

[Screenshot 4: Coaching]
AI Sales Coach - Personalized recommendations per deal

‚Üí Watch 2-min product demo: acmeanalytics.com/demo
```

**Design Tips**:
- High-resolution screenshots
- Zoom in on key areas
- Annotate with arrows and labels
- Keep text minimal
- Show real data (not fake/lorem ipsum)

**Common Mistakes**:
- Too many screenshots (cluttered)
- Low-quality or outdated images
- No annotations or context
- Showing features, not value
- Forgetting mobile or key platforms

### Slide 6: Business Model

**Purpose**: Explain how you make money and the path to profitability

**Structure**:
1. Revenue model (subscription, transaction, etc.)
2. Pricing strategy and tiers
3. Customer acquisition motion
4. Unit economics (CAC, LTV, gross margin)
5. Revenue streams (current and planned)

**Revenue Models**:
- **SaaS**: Subscription (monthly/annual)
- **Marketplace**: Transaction fees or commission
- **Freemium**: Free tier ‚Üí paid upgrade
- **Usage-based**: Pay per usage/consumption
- **Hybrid**: Combination of models

**Pricing Strategy**:
- Show pricing tiers (good, better, best)
- Annual vs. monthly pricing
- Price anchoring and packaging
- Expansion revenue opportunities

**Example (SaaS)**:
```
BUSINESS MODEL: SaaS subscription with seat-based pricing

Pricing Tiers:
‚Ä¢ Starter: $99/user/month (5-20 users)
‚Ä¢ Professional: $149/user/month (21-100 users)
‚Ä¢ Enterprise: Custom pricing (100+ users)

Annual prepay: 20% discount

Customer Acquisition:
‚Ä¢ Product-led growth (PLG): Free trial ‚Üí paid conversion
‚Ä¢ Inside sales: Inbound leads + outbound prospecting
‚Ä¢ Partnerships: Integration marketplace partners

Unit Economics (at scale):
‚Ä¢ CAC: $1,200 (payback in 8 months)
‚Ä¢ LTV: $7,500 (3-year average)
‚Ä¢ LTV/CAC: 6.2x
‚Ä¢ Gross Margin: 82%

Revenue Streams:
‚Ä¢ Subscriptions: 85% (recurring)
‚Ä¢ Professional services: 10% (implementation)
‚Ä¢ Marketplace commissions: 5% (integration partners)
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Pricing table or tiers
- Revenue mix pie chart
- Funnel visualization (PLG motion)
- Unit economics comparison

**Common Mistakes**:
- No clear monetization strategy
- Pricing too low or too high
- Ignoring competition's pricing
- No expansion revenue plan
- Missing unit economics

### Slide 7: Traction

**Purpose**: Prove momentum, validation, and product-market fit

**Structure**:
1. Key metric over time (growth chart)
2. Current metrics snapshot
3. Growth rate (MoM, YoY)
4. Customer logos or testimonials
5. Key milestones achieved

**What to Show (by Stage)**:

**Pre-Seed**:
- User interviews completed
- Waitlist signups
- Pilot customers
- Product launches
- Early feedback/NPS

**Seed**:
- MRR/ARR growth chart
- User/customer count
- Growth rate (% MoM)
- Key customer wins
- Product-market fit signals

**Series A**:
- $1M+ ARR
- Strong growth (10-20% MoM)
- Customer logos
- Retention metrics
- Unit economics improving

**Metrics to Highlight**:
- **SaaS**: MRR, ARR, customer count, NRR, churn
- **Marketplace**: GMV, take rate, supply/demand balance
- **Consumer**: MAU, DAU, engagement, retention
- **Enterprise**: Contract value, pipeline, win rate

**Example (Seed Stage SaaS)**:
```

MRR Growth: [Chart showing $0 ‚Üí $70k over 18 months]

Current Metrics (October 2025):
‚Ä¢ $70k MRR ($840k ARR run rate)
‚Ä¢ 420 paying customers
‚Ä¢ 22% MoM growth (avg last 6 months)
‚Ä¢ 96% gross revenue retention
‚Ä¢ 115% net revenue retention (expansion)
‚Ä¢ 35% free-to-paid conversion

Customer Logos:
[Logos of 6-8 recognizable customers]

Milestones:
‚úì Product launch - March 2024
‚úì First paying customer - May 2024
‚úì $10k MRR - September 2024
‚úì $50k MRR - June 2025
‚úì 100+ customers - August 2025
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Growth chart (hockey stick preferred)
- Metric dashboard
- Customer logos
- Testimonial quote with headshot
- Milestone timeline

**Common Mistakes**:
- Vanity metrics (downloads, signups) instead of revenue
- No growth rate shown
- Cherry-picking favorable timeframes
- Missing customer validation
- Outdated metrics (> 1 month old)

### Slide 8: Competition

**Purpose**: Show you understand the market and have clear differentiation

**Structure**:
1. Competitive landscape visualization
2. Direct competitors
3. Indirect competitors
4. Alternative (status quo / do nothing)

- X-axis: Feature/capability (e.g., "Ease of Use")
- Y-axis: Feature/capability (e.g., "AI Capabilities")

- You should be in top-right quadrant

**Competitive Table**:
- Rows: Key features or capabilities
- Columns: You and 3-4 main competitors
- Checkmarks for feature presence
- You should have more checkmarks

- Position competitors by category

### Slide 9: Competitive Advantage / Moat

**Purpose**: Explain why you'll win long-term and build sustainable defensibility

**Structure**:
1. Unfair advantage statement
2. Moat categories and your strengths
3. Barriers to entry you're building
4. Network effects or data advantages
5. Strategic assets (IP, partnerships, team)

**Moat Categories**:

**1. Technology / IP**
- Proprietary algorithms or AI models
- Patents (filed or granted)
- Technical architecture advantages
- Years of R&D investment
- Unique data or training sets

**2. Network Effects**
- More users = more value
- Data network effects (more data = better product)
- Platform effects (ecosystem of partners)
- Marketplace effects (supply & demand)

**3. Data Advantages**
- Unique data sources
- Proprietary datasets
- Data flywheel (usage ‚Üí data ‚Üí better product)
- First-mover data advantage

**4. Brand and Distribution**
- Category creation/ownership
- Strong brand recognition
- Distribution partnerships
- Customer lock-in and switching costs

**5. Team and Expertise**
- Deep domain expertise
- Unique founder insights
- World-class team from [companies]
- Advisor network

**Example**:
```
COMPETITIVE ADVANTAGE: AI data flywheel and domain expertise

1. Proprietary AI Models
‚Ä¢ 3+ years training on 50M+ sales conversations

2. Data Network Effects
‚Ä¢ More customers ‚Üí more sales conversation data
‚Ä¢ More data ‚Üí better AI predictions

‚Ä¢ Currently: 50M+ conversations, growing 2M+/month

‚Ä¢ CEO: 10+ years in sales, former Salesforce VP

‚Ä¢ Deep CRM integration (3-6 months to switch)

```

**Visual Elements**:
- Data flywheel diagram
- Patent portfolio summary
- Partnership logos
- Team pedigree (company logos)

**Common Mistakes**:
- No clear, defensible moat
- Moat is just "great team" or "execution"
- Easily replicable advantage
- No evidence or proof points
- Ignoring competitive threats

### Slide 10: Go-to-Market Strategy

**Purpose**: Show you know how to acquire and retain customers profitably

**Structure**:
1. Target customer segments (prioritized)
2. Customer acquisition channels
3. Sales and marketing playbook
4. Partnerships and distribution
5. Customer success and retention strategy

**Customer Segmentation**:
- **ICP (Ideal Customer Profile)**: Company size, industry, revenue
- **Persona**: Decision maker, title, pain points
- **Segments**: Prioritized by fit and economics

**Acquisition Channels**:

**Product-Led Growth (PLG)**:
- Free trial or freemium model
- Viral mechanics or referrals
- Self-service onboarding
- In-product upsells

**Sales-Led Growth**:
- Inbound leads (content, SEO, paid)
- Outbound prospecting (SDRs)
- Field sales (enterprise)
- Channel partners

**Hybrid Approach**:
- PLG for SMB, sales-led for enterprise
- Free trial ‚Üí sales outreach for high-value
- Community-led growth ‚Üí paid plans

**Example**:
```
GO-TO-MARKET: Product-led growth for SMB, sales-led for enterprise

Target Segments (in priority order):
1. Growth-stage B2B SaaS (primary)
   ‚Ä¢ 50-500 employees, $10M-$100M ARR
   ‚Ä¢ 10-100 sales reps
   ‚Ä¢ Using Salesforce or HubSpot

2. Tech-forward enterprises (secondary)
   ‚Ä¢ 500-2,000 employees
   ‚Ä¢ 100+ sales reps
   ‚Ä¢ Existing sales tech stack

Customer Acquisition:
‚Ä¢ Content marketing: SEO, thought leadership
‚Ä¢ Product-led: 14-day free trial, no credit card
‚Ä¢ Inbound sales: Lead scoring, inside sales team
‚Ä¢ Outbound: SDR team targeting ICP
‚Ä¢ Partnerships: Salesforce, HubSpot app stores

Sales Process:
‚Ä¢ SMB (< $10k ACV): Self-service + inside sales (30-day cycle)
‚Ä¢ Mid-market ($10-50k ACV): Inside sales + AE (60-day cycle)
‚Ä¢ Enterprise ($50k+ ACV): Field sales + SE (90-120 day cycle)

Retention Strategy:
‚Ä¢ Onboarding: Dedicated CSM for $20k+ accounts
‚Ä¢ Product: Continuous feature releases, high engagement
‚Ä¢ Support: 24/7 chat, 1-hour response SLA
‚Ä¢ Expansion: Usage-based upsells, new modules
```

**Visual Elements**:
- Customer acquisition funnel
- Channel mix (pie chart)
- Sales process flow
- Retention cohort chart

**Common Mistakes**:
- Too many channels (lack of focus)
- No clear ICP or segmentation
- Acquisition strategy doesn't match product
- Missing retention/expansion plan
- No proof channel works (if early stage)

### Slide 12: Financials

**Purpose**: Show historical performance and realistic future projections

**Structure**:
1. Historical revenue and key metrics (2-3 years)
2. Projected revenue and growth (3-5 years)
3. Unit economics and margins
4. Key assumptions driving growth
5. Path to profitability

**What to Show**:

**Historical (2-3 years)**:
- Monthly or quarterly revenue (actual)
- Customer count
- Key metric progression
- Milestone achievements

**Projections (3-5 years)**:
- Annual revenue forecast
- Customer count
- Team size
- Key assumptions

**Unit Economics**:
- Gross margin (current and projected)
- CAC and LTV
- CAC payback period
- Burn multiple ($ burned / $ ARR added)

- Strategic acquisition value

- Market opportunity size

## Design Best Practices

### Visual Design

**Layout and Spacing**:
- Consistent margins and padding
- Plenty of white space
- Grid-based layout for alignment
- One key message per slide

**Typography**:
- 2-3 fonts maximum
- Large, readable font sizes (24pt+ for body)
- Consistent hierarchy (headings, subheadings, body)
- High contrast (dark text on light background)

**Colors**:
- Brand-aligned color palette
- 2-3 primary colors maximum
- High contrast for readability
- Consistent color usage throughout

**Images and Graphics**:
- High-resolution images (300 DPI+)
- Authentic photos (not cheesy stock photos)
- Icons for visual interest
- Charts and graphs for data
- Screenshots for product visualization

**Data Visualization**:
- Simple, clean charts
- Annotate key data points
- Use color to highlight insights
- Avoid 3D or complex chart types
- Label axes clearly

### Content Best Practices

**Slide Titles**:
- Clear, action-oriented
- One key message per slide
- Use sentence case or title case (be consistent)
- Bold and prominent

**Body Text**:
- Bullets, not paragraphs
- 3-5 bullets per slide maximum
- Short, punchy sentences
- Active voice
- Quantify everything

**Storytelling**:
- Clear narrative arc (problem ‚Üí solution ‚Üí traction ‚Üí team ‚Üí ask)
- Build momentum throughout
- Connect slides logically
- Use customer stories and examples
- End with inspiring vision

### Format and Distribution

**File Formats**:
- **PowerPoint (.pptx)**: For presenting and editing
- **PDF**: For email distribution (password-protected)
- **DocSend**: For tracking analytics (opens, time per slide)
- **Video**: Recorded pitch for asynchronous viewing

**Naming Convention**:
```
CompanyName_PitchDeck_Stage_Date.pdf

Examples:
AcmeAnalytics_SeedDeck_Oct2025.pdf
AcmeAnalytics_SeedDeck_Oct2025_Confidential.pdf
```

**Version Control**:
- Date every version
- Track changes in filename
- Keep master in Google Drive/Dropbox
- Archive old versions

**Password Protection**:
- Always password-protect PDFs
- Use simple password (company name + round)
- Include password in email signature
- Example: "Password: AcmeSeed2025"

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Content Errors
1. **Too much text**: Use bullets, not paragraphs
2. **Weak problem**: Problem not painful or clear enough
3. **No traction**: Missing key metrics or validation
4. **Ignoring competition**: Claiming no competitors
5. **Unrealistic projections**: 10x growth with no basis
6. **Weak team**: Missing key roles or experience
7. **Vague use of funds**: No detail on how money is spent
8. **No clear ask**: Missing fundraising amount or terms

### Design Errors
1. **Cluttered slides**: Too much information per slide
2. **Inconsistent design**: Different fonts, colors, styles
3. **Low-quality images**: Pixelated or stretched images
4. **Poor contrast**: Hard to read text
5. **Complex charts**: 3D charts or too much data
6. **Cheesy stock photos**: Inauthentic imagery
7. **Too many slides**: > 20 slides (15 is ideal)

### Strategic Errors
1. **Solution-first**: Pitching solution before problem
2. **Feature-focused**: Talking features, not benefits
3. **Missing "why now"**: No market timing or catalyst
4. **Weak differentiation**: Not 10x better than alternatives
5. **No moat**: Easily replicable advantage
6. **Wrong stage**: Metrics don't match round (seed vs. Series A)
7. **Poor storytelling**: Disjointed narrative
