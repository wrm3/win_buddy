# Non-Profit Formation Skill

Comprehensive reference for forming and operating non-profit organizations, religious entities, and political organizations in the USA. This skill supports the `nonprofit-specialist` agent.

## ⚠️ Important Disclaimer

**This skill provides educational information only, not legal or tax advice.**
- Consult a non-profit attorney for legal matters
- Consult a CPA experienced in non-profit accounting
- IRS rules are complex and change frequently
- State requirements vary significantly

## 501(c) Organization Types

### Comparison Table

| Type | Purpose | Tax-Deductible Donations | Political Activity | Lobbying |
|------|---------|--------------------------|-------------------|----------|
| 501(c)(3) | Charitable, religious, educational | Yes | Prohibited | Limited |
| 501(c)(4) | Social welfare | No | Allowed (not primary) | Unlimited |
| 501(c)(6) | Business leagues | No | Limited | Allowed |
| 527 | Political | No | Primary purpose | N/A |

### 501(c)(3) - Charitable Organizations

**Eligible Purposes:**
- Religious
- Charitable
- Scientific
- Literary
- Educational
- Prevention of cruelty to children/animals
- Public safety testing

**Key Restrictions:**
- No private inurement (no profits to individuals)
- No substantial lobbying
- No political campaign activity
- Must serve public interest

### 501(c)(4) - Social Welfare Organizations

**Eligible Purposes:**
- Promote social welfare
- Civic improvement
- Community betterment

**Key Features:**
- Can engage in political activity (not primary purpose)
- Unlimited lobbying allowed
- Donors not publicly disclosed
- Donations not tax-deductible

## Formation Process

### 501(c)(3) Formation Timeline

```
Week 1-2: Planning
├── Define mission and programs
├── Identify board members (min 3)
├── Choose state of incorporation
└── Select name (check availability)

Week 3-4: State Formation
├── File Articles of Incorporation
├── Include required IRS language
├── Pay state filing fee ($50-300)
└── Obtain EIN (free, immediate)

Week 5-6: Governance
├── Draft bylaws
├── Hold organizational meeting
├── Elect officers
└── Open bank account

Week 7-12: IRS Application
├── Prepare Form 1023 or 1023-EZ
├── Gather required documents
├── Pay IRS fee ($275-600)
└── Submit application

Month 3-6: IRS Review
├── Respond to any IRS questions
├── Receive determination letter
└── Begin operations as 501(c)(3)
```

### Required IRS Language (Articles of Incorporation)

```
ARTICLE [X] - PURPOSE

This corporation is organized exclusively for [charitable, 
religious, educational, scientific] purposes within the 
meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code.

ARTICLE [X] - LIMITATIONS

No part of the net earnings of the corporation shall inure 
to the benefit of, or be distributable to its members, 
trustees, officers, or other private persons, except that 
the corporation shall be authorized and empowered to pay 
reasonable compensation for services rendered.

No substantial part of the activities of the corporation 
shall be the carrying on of propaganda, or otherwise 
attempting to influence legislation, and the corporation 
shall not participate in, or intervene in (including the 
publishing or distribution of statements) any political 
campaign on behalf of (or in opposition to) any candidate 
for public office.

ARTICLE [X] - DISSOLUTION

Upon the dissolution of the corporation, assets shall be 
distributed for one or more exempt purposes within the 
meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, 
or to the federal government, or to a state or local 
government, for a public purpose.
```

### Form 1023 vs 1023-EZ

| Criteria | Form 1023-EZ | Form 1023 |
|----------|--------------|-----------|
| Projected revenue | ≤$50K/year (3 years) | >$50K/year |
| Total assets | ≤$250K | >$250K |
| Fee | $275 | $600 |
| Processing time | 2-4 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Complexity | Simple | Detailed |

**1023-EZ Ineligible:**
- Schools
- Hospitals
- Supporting organizations
- Foreign organizations
- Credit counseling

## Religious Organizations

### Church vs Religious Non-Profit

| Feature | Church | Religious Non-Profit |
|---------|--------|---------------------|
| IRS Application | Not required | Required (Form 1023) |
| Form 990 | Not required | Required |
| Automatic exemption | Yes | No |
| IRS audit protection | Yes (special rules) | Standard |

### IRS Church Criteria (14 Points)

```
Not all required, but IRS considers:

□ Distinct religious history
□ Distinct religious beliefs
□ Recognized creed and form of worship
□ Formal code of doctrine
□ Distinct ecclesiastical government
□ Ordained ministers
□ Minister selection process
□ Literature of its own
□ Established places of worship
□ Regular congregations
□ Regular religious services
□ Sunday schools for children
□ Schools for ministers
□ Membership not associated with other churches

Most legitimate churches meet 8+ criteria.
```

### Minister Tax Benefits

```
Parsonage Allowance (Housing):
- Tax-free housing allowance
- Must be designated in advance
- Limited to fair rental value
- Only for ordained ministers

SECA Exemption (Form 4361):
- Can opt out of Social Security
- Must be conscientiously opposed
- Irrevocable decision
- Applies to ministerial income only
```

## Political Organizations

### PAC Types

| Type | Contribution Limits | Coordination | Disclosure |
|------|---------------------|--------------|------------|
| Traditional PAC | Yes ($5K/candidate) | Yes | Full |
| Super PAC | No limits | No | Full |
| Hybrid PAC | Both accounts | Separated | Full |

### 501(c)(4) Political Activity Rules

```
Primary Purpose Test:
- Political activity cannot be primary purpose
- Generally interpreted as <50%
- Measure by time, money, or both

Allowed Activities:
✓ Voter registration (nonpartisan)
✓ Issue advocacy
✓ Lobbying (unlimited)
✓ Candidate ratings/scorecards
✓ Get-out-the-vote (nonpartisan)

Prohibited/Limited:
✗ Express advocacy as primary purpose
✗ Coordination with campaigns
✗ Contribution to candidates
```

### FEC Reporting Requirements

```
Quarterly Reports (Form 3X):
- Due 15th day after quarter end
- All receipts and disbursements
- Itemize contributions >$200

Pre-Election Reports:
- 12 days before election
- All activity since last report

24/48 Hour Reports:
- Large contributions near election
- Independent expenditures >$10K
```

## Compliance

### Annual Filing Requirements

| Organization | Annual Filing | Deadline |
|--------------|---------------|----------|
| 501(c)(3) <$50K | Form 990-N (e-Postcard) | 15th day, 5th month |
| 501(c)(3) $50K-$200K | Form 990-EZ | 15th day, 5th month |
| 501(c)(3) >$200K | Form 990 | 15th day, 5th month |
| Church | None required | N/A |
| 501(c)(4) | Form 990 | 15th day, 5th month |
| PAC | FEC reports | Quarterly + pre-election |

### Form 990 Key Sections

```
Part I: Summary
- Mission, activities, governance

Part III: Program Accomplishments
- Description of programs
- Expenses and achievements

Part VII: Compensation
- Officers, directors, key employees
- Highest compensated employees

Part VIII: Revenue
- Contributions, grants
- Program service revenue
- Investment income

Part IX: Expenses
- Functional expenses
- Program vs admin vs fundraising

Schedule A: Public Charity Status
- Public support test
- Type of public charity

Schedule B: Contributors
- Major donors (confidential)
- Required if contributions >$5K
```

### Public Support Test (501(c)(3))

```
To maintain public charity status:

1/3 Support Test:
- At least 33.33% of support from:
  - Public contributions
  - Government grants
  - Program service revenue

Facts and Circumstances Test:
- At least 10% public support
- Plus other favorable factors

Failure = Private Foundation status
(more restrictions, 5% distribution requirement)
```

## State Requirements

### Common State Requirements

```
□ State incorporation
□ Registered agent
□ Annual report
□ State tax exemption application
□ Charitable solicitation registration
□ Sales tax exemption
□ Property tax exemption
```

### Charitable Solicitation Registration

```
Required in ~40 states if:
- Soliciting donations from that state's residents
- Even online fundraising counts

Unified Registration Statement (URS):
- Accepted by ~36 states
- Single form, multiple filings
- Still pay separate fees

Exemptions vary by state:
- Churches often exempt
- Small organizations (<$25K)
- Organizations with no paid fundraisers
```

## Governance

### Board Best Practices

```
Composition:
- Minimum 3 members (most states)
- No family majority
- Diverse skills and backgrounds
- Independent members

Meetings:
- At least annual (quarterly recommended)
- Document minutes
- Quorum requirements

Duties:
- Duty of Care (informed decisions)
- Duty of Loyalty (no conflicts)
- Duty of Obedience (follow mission)
```

### Conflict of Interest Policy

```markdown
# Conflict of Interest Policy

## Purpose
Protect the organization's interest when contemplating 
transactions that might benefit private interests.

## Definitions
- Interested Person: Board member, officer, or key employee
- Financial Interest: Direct or indirect ownership, 
  compensation, or potential ownership

## Procedures
1. Disclosure of potential conflicts
2. Determining if conflict exists
3. Addressing the conflict
4. Violations of policy

## Annual Statements
Each board member shall annually sign a statement:
- Affirming receipt of policy
- Agreeing to comply
- Disclosing known interests
```

### Document Retention

```
Permanent:
- Articles of Incorporation
- Bylaws
- IRS determination letter
- Board minutes
- Tax returns (Form 990)

7 Years:
- Financial records
- Contracts
- Employment records
- Donor records

3 Years:
- General correspondence
- Bank statements
- Receipts
```

## Financial Management

### Functional Expense Allocation

```
Expenses must be allocated to:

Program Services (target: 75%+)
- Direct program costs
- Staff time on programs
- Materials for programs

Management & General (target: <15%)
- Executive oversight
- Accounting
- Board expenses

Fundraising (target: <15%)
- Solicitation costs
- Events
- Grant writing

Watchdog ratings often look at:
- Program expense ratio >75%
- Fundraising efficiency <$0.25 per $1 raised
```

### Unrelated Business Income (UBIT)

```
Taxable if:
1. Trade or business
2. Regularly carried on
3. Not substantially related to exempt purpose

Common UBIT sources:
- Advertising revenue
- Rental income (with services)
- Merchandise sales (unrelated)
- Sponsorship (if quid pro quo)

Exemptions:
- Volunteer labor
- Donated merchandise
- Passive income (dividends, interest)
- Royalties
```
